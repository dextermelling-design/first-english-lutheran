Faith on a journey

What we believe

Central convictions that hold us together as followers of Jesus.

Not every Lutheran Christian holds exactly the same beliefs — and among the members of First English Lutheran Church, there are a host of different perspectives. Ultimately, the nature and ways of God are beyond our understanding, so we consider ourselves people of faith on a journey of understanding. Of course, there are some central beliefs that hold us together as followers of Jesus.

God

We believe in a God who is known in three persons — the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. This understanding of the Trinity reveals a God who is, in essence, defined by relationship.

  • The Father — source of all that is, creator and sustainer of the universe; that creative action has always been shared with the Son and Holy Spirit.
  • The Son — Jesus Christ became human in history. The love of God was revealed as Jesus suffered and died so that we might know God’s love has no limits.
  • The Holy Spirit — God’s abiding presence in the world, joining us in prayer and worship and empowering us to live as God’s people in love and service.

Bible

  • The Bible has the power to shape and transform our lives each and every day.
  • God’s Holy Spirit inspired the writers of the Bible to reveal truth about God.
  • The importance of the scriptures is not only whether facts are reported accurately, but what we learn about our relationship with God and one another.
  • In preaching and teaching, pastors and others help us engage the Bible more fully — but no human being has absolute authority to determine a single meaning from scripture.
  • How a text interacts with the rest of scripture — especially the teaching of Jesus — matters greatly in our understanding of God’s truth.

Worship

Weekly worship expresses our honor and directs our praise to God, who alone is worthy of our devotion. Worshiping together makes us accountable to something beyond ourselves. We are actively engaged: singing, praying, reading texts responsively, and sharing in Holy Communion.

We believe there are many styles of worship — at FELC we offer both traditional and contemporary styles. Giving tithes and offerings expresses our gratitude and trust while fueling the work of the congregation.

Worship follows an ancient four-fold pattern:

Gather

We come together in prayer and praise.

Word

We read scriptures and hear a message.

Meal

We share in the gift of Holy Communion.

Sending

We prepare to go out and serve others.

Serving others

Jesus taught about God’s love by caring for others, teaching that he came not to be served but to serve. As people who strive to follow Jesus’ example, we believe that serving others is a primary means of living our faith.

Core values

  • The Word of God is alive!
  • We welcome all!
  • Worship is centered in Word & Sacrament.
  • We practice faith in everyday life.
  • We are honest about brokenness.
  • God has created us for connection and relationship.

First English Lutheran Church is a member of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America (ELCA).
